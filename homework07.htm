Background

Two numbers, \(i\) and \(j\) are said to be amicable pairs if the sum of the proper factors of \(i\) gives you \(j\), and the sum of the factors of \(j\) gives you \(i\). For example, 220 and 284 are amicable pairs because the sum of the proper factors of 220 is:

\(1 + 2 + 4 + 5 + 10 + 11 + 22 + 44 + 55 + 110 = 284\)

and the sum of the factors of 284 is:

\(1 + 2 + 4 + 71 + 142 = 220\)

Numbers which are amicable pairs with themselves are called perfect numbers. For example, 6 is a perfect number because the sum of the proper factors of 6 are:

\(1 + 2 + 3 = 6\)

Requirements

You are to write a program that finds all amicable pairs less than 200,000. You must do this as follows:

  1. Create a vector, \(v\), of size 200,000.
  2. Fill each element of the vector with the sum of the factors of the index. \[v_i = \sum_{1 \le f \lt i}^{i \% f == 0} f \]
  3. Look for numbers \(i\) and \(j\) such that \(v_i = j\) and \(v_j = i\), and print them out.

You should parallelize step 2: calculating the sum of the factors. Then you should time this step using different scheduling algorithms. In particular, you should use

  • Block scheduling
  • Block interleaved scheduling, with blocks of 10,000
  • Block interleaved scheduling, with blocks of 100
  • Dynamic scheduling, with blocks of size 10,000
  • Dynamic scheduling, with blocks of size 100
